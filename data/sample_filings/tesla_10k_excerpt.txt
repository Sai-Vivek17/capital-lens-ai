Tesla revenue is generated primarily from automotive sales and leasing, regulatory credits, energy generation and storage, services, and other products. Automotive gross margin can be affected by pricing actions, raw material costs, production ramps, warranty costs, and product mix.

The electric vehicle market is increasingly competitive as global automakers and new entrants expand EV offerings. Tesla must continue reducing costs, improving manufacturing efficiency, and differentiating through software, charging, brand, and autonomy capabilities.

Tesla's strategy includes energy storage, charging infrastructure, autonomous driving systems, robotics, and software-enabled services. Many of these opportunities depend on technical execution, regulatory approval, safety performance, and customer adoption.

The company is exposed to volatility in demand, interest rates, consumer credit conditions, commodity prices, and geopolitical factors. Leadership attention, reputational issues, and regulatory investigations can also influence investor perception.
