Apple derives revenue primarily from iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables, Home and Accessories, and Services. Services include advertising, AppleCare, cloud services, digital content, payment services, and licensing.

The company believes its integrated ecosystem, brand loyalty, installed base, and developer platform are important competitive advantages. Demand can be affected by product launch timing, macroeconomic conditions, foreign exchange rates, and consumer replacement cycles.

Apple faces substantial competition in smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, services, and digital content. The company also faces regulatory scrutiny related to platform access, App Store policies, privacy, payments, and competition law.

Supply chain concentration is a risk because many components and finished products depend on partners in Asia. Disruption from geopolitics, logistics, public health, or supplier financial distress could affect product availability and margins.
